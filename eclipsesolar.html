IN THE SHADOW OF MOON: EXPLORING SOLAR ECLIPSE

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between the Earth and the Sun, obscuring all or part of the Sun's light. This celestial event happens only during a new moon, when the Sun and Moon are in perfect alignment as seen from Earth

There are three main types of solar eclipses: total, partial, and annular. During a total solar eclipse, the Sun is completely covered by the Moon, revealing the Sun's corona. Partial solar eclipses occur when only a portion of the Sun is obscured, while annular eclipses happen when the Moon is farther from Earth, appearing smaller and not completely covering the Sun, resulting in a ring of sunlight around the darkened Moon. Solar eclipses offer unique opportunities for scientific observation and awe-inspiring experiences for observers around the world.On average, there are between 2 to 5 solar eclipses in a year. The frequency of solar eclipses can vary due to factors such as the tilt of the Earth's axis and the eccentricity of the Moon's orbit around the Earth.
