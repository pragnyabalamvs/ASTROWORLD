THE MOON
CHANDRA
From lighting up our skies to maintaining a geological record of our solar system's history, Earth's closest celestial neighbor plays a pivotal role in the studies of our planet and our solar system. The Moon's gravitational pull causes ocean tides on Earth, known as tidal forces.The Moon's gravity is about one-sixth that of Earth's.The Moon exhibits different phases as it orbits Earth, caused by the changing angles of sunlight illuminating its surface.

2024 TOTAL ECLIPSE
WHEN AND WHERE
On April 8, 2024, a total solar eclipse will cross North America, passing over Mexico, United States, and Canada.The duration of totality will vary depending on the location along the path. In some areas, totality may last for over four minutes.Visibility of the eclipse will depend on weather conditions in your location.

THE SUN
HELIOS
The Sun is the star at the heart of our solar system. Its gravity holds the solar system together, keeping everything - from the biggest planets to the smallest bits of debris - in its orbit.It is composed mainly of hydrogen(about 74%).The sun's energy is produced through nuclear fusion.
ASTRAL
SKYWATCHING
STARGAZING
It refers to the act of observing celestial phenomena, such as stars, planets, the Moon, meteors, and other celestial objects, typically done with the naked eye, binoculars or telescopes. Skywatching allows us to connect with the vastness of the cosmos, reminding us of our place in the universe.It is a popular hobby enjoyed by people of all ages and backgrounds who have an interest in astronomy and the natural world.
