Myths and Legends of Solar and Lunar Eclipses

Exploring the Mysteries of the Cosmos

The Dragon Devouring the Sun

In ancient Chinese mythology, a solar eclipse was often interpreted as a fearsome dragon devouring the Sun. To ward off this celestial beast, people would make loud noises, play drums, and shoot arrows into the sky.

The Cosmic Battle

Among Hindu myths, eclipses were believed to occur during a cosmic battle between the demon Rahu and the god Surya (the Sun). When Rahu swallowed the Sun or the Moon, an eclipse was said to occur. To prevent this, prayers and rituals were performed to protect the Sun and Moon.

The Bear and the Sun

Indigenous North American cultures have their own eclipse myths. Some Native American tribes saw a celestial bear attacking the Sun or the Moon during an eclipse. This belief led to ceremonies and storytelling to ensure the Sun or Moon's safety.

The Darkening of the Moon

Across various cultures, lunar eclipses were often associated with the Moon being eaten or darkened by a supernatural being. Rituals and ceremonies were performed to protect the Moon and restore its light.
